I had something else in mind for my first post, but right now I think it is more important to explain, mostly to myself, why I want to write.

I have never enjoyed writing. I think a lot of people like me can relate to the dread of doing things they are not particularly interested in. I feel this a lot of the time at my day job.

Unlike my super cool side projects, which I’ll talk more about soon, I have always struggled with motivation when I feel like my efforts are being wasted on something unimportant or inefficient, or when I feel like I am not being listened to. Whether this is just a failure on my end or not, it has led to me having one of the most opinionated personalities I know of.

I really do not care at this point. I know I am supposed to grow and change, but I think this is one part of my personality that I am pretty secure in.

Back on topic. I want to write because, over time, through my job, my friends, and my life in general, I have developed opinions that people sometimes come around to. Not that I think I am some sort of [larper](https://www.urbandictionary.com/define.php?term=Larper), but being able to accept this has led me to the greatest friend network I have ever had. I just had to be myself.

Recently, I have had a lot of free time at work. This has led me to read and consume a lot of media. I have to thank two of my friends at work for putting me onto [Hacker News](https://news.ycombinator.com) and [lobste.rs](https://lobste.rs), because they have allowed me to consume more slop than AI TikTok videos. More so the orange one.

It has been funny to see how such garbage takes can make it onto the front page. We started discussing this at work, and recently we took it upon ourselves to learn and use plain text email. 

> [*Use Plain Text Email Please!*](https://useplaintext.email)

Anyway, starting with coffee runs, we have begun corresponding about these particular blogs, thoughts, and ideas. Some of the brief chats that we have feel more put together than half of the Primagen’s videography. Not to say we are elevated, but we are not catering to an audience and we are not SF startup losers.

If anything, it has made me realise how important it is to write ideas down. I will probably look back on this and cringe, but that is kind of the point.

So I feel the need now to express what exactly my goals are.

1. Track my ideas and opinions over time, and see how much they change.
2. Improve my writing skills and not rely on writing aids for grammar, prose, and clarity.
3. Take time to reflect on life while I am writing.
4. Do something that has no direct reward for me or anyone else.

Honestly, this may never reach another reader. I think before, I would have stopped doing this before getting to this stage. I am pretty proud of myself for getting this far, to be honest.
